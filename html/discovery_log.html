Discovery Log: WEBF1

Will Green: UP853829

September 2017

Abstract

In this context, a discovery log is used as an environment to document what I have completed or achieved throughout WEBF1 practicals.

Introduction

Starting University during September of 2017, one of my units requires us to journal a discovery log, I prefer the term learning log because I don't necessarily learn in every lecture or practical.

Logs

In this section, I will give details to what was achieved throughout first year practicals for WEBF1. This "Discovery Log" of mine will be integrated with one of the courseworks set, I believe it is to turn this log into HTML pages.

Week Zero: 18th - 24th Sept

Today; on Friday 22/09/17, We were introduced to our lecturers and the course; however, it was not an actual practical. As part of the introduction week, we simply went over how to use Moodle for our Module and what is expected of us.

Week One: 25th - 1st Sept/ Oct

Today; on Friday 29/09/17, I had my first formal WEBF1 practical of the academic year, this entailed a large majority of the session being dedicated to introductions between lectures and fellow peers, it also allowed the class to be informed of any introductory projects we are going to have. Introductory segments were allocated to the overview of the unit, breaking down each module to a few areas:

  • Performance for each module.
  • What each module contains.
  • What is expected of us.
  • Work-load.

As web-dev goes; it is axiomatic that servers are involved, the University has generously paid for and provided the details for each student to own a domain on Google’s cloud servers, we have access to a personal virtual environment to perform task that may aid our work. To which, I installed Metasploit.

Week Two: 2nd - 8th Oct

Today; Friday 06/10/17, As a class, we had the task to individually index and locate websites as part of a questionnaire, we had to find “horrific” websites such as: www.lingscars.com or www.hosanna1.com. Other questions related to similar areas; however, differed to examples such as: websites that I favored or disliked, the second part of this task was to justify why I liked or disliked each URL.

Week Three: 9th - 15th Oct

Today; Friday 13/10/17, We were tasked to work in groups of three to four people to complete a Google Doc based on Web Security, we had five sections to input data, mainly opinions, the subject headers were:

  • Information generated about myself on a daily basis.
  • How it is captured.
  • How it is shared.
  • How it can be secured.

We had to list several ways of how information is created regarding oneself on mediums like social media. We concluded how much personal data people upload on a daily basis, this can be shocking because of how much information third party people can gather. This can be, geolocation via sign in and photos to learn personal patters and whereabouts, possibility of leading to criminal actions. To end the lesson, we had one class Doc as a public preference for the entirety of information to be shared to compare and evaluate student answers.

Today; Friday 20/10/17, the main focus for this week is coursework, this is because our first draft is due on the 3rd... seeing as the date is the 27th, yeah, you get the idea. We have been given a task sheet to complete labelled ``Better Writing''.
